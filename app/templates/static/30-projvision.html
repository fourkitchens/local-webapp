Project vision

We believe CGAP and the Microfinance Gateway can advance financial knowledge and access across the globe, ultimately lifting people out of poverty. As an interactive hub of information exchange, the future site will be an invaluable resource for those involved in the microfinance community, empowering all with relevant, timely, and actionable knowledge.

Four Kitchens will work closely with CGAP to design the information architecture, wireframes, user experience across all devices, and technical and nontechnical requirements for merging the four Gateway sites into a single Drupal platform and database, all while allowing editorial independence for the regional sites, supporting existing languages, and laying a strong foundation to easily expanding language support in the future. To achieve this, our work will focus on the following goals:

  1. Strengthen the libraries by designing better interfaces and editorial workflows for adding and editing content.
  2. Reframe the jobs and events offerings by increasing user engagement. This can be achieved by allowing users and organizations to create accounts — perhaps allowing users to affiliate themselves with organizations — and set alerts for specific types of jobs and events postings.
  3. Unify the disparate language sites into a single, multilingual site that will be able to accommodate new languages as the organization continues to grow.
  4. Grow community engagement by adding content recommendation and sharing tools, as well as social media integration. While these tactics will help increase traffic, we need to create an excellent and engaging user experience that will keep visitors on the site (as measured by time spent on the site or number of pageviews per session). This can be achieved, in part, by creating a mobile-first, responsive design that works on all devices. As more people use phones and tablets as their primary browsing and reading tools — often leaving their laptops on the couch next to them, unopened — sites must provide an excellent mobile experience, or visitors will quickly grow annoyed with a site and move on.
  5. Strengthen search and content discoverability by adding a robust, multilingual taxonomy, browsing by content type or topic, and excellent keyword and faceted search functionality across all languages.
