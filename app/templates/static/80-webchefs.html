Meet the Web Chefs

While this is not a comprehensive list of our top-notch developers, designers, and support staff, the following Web Chefs will likely be assigned to the Microfinance Gateway project.

Todd Ross Nienkerk

Partner, Co-Founder, Digital Strategist
Todd Nienkerk has been involved in the web design and publishing industries since 1996. Since that time, he has designed and built countless websites and written for, edited, and managed several online and print publications. Although Todd's interests focus primarily on visual design and user experience, his strong background in development allows him to contribute to virtually any creative or technical task. As an active member of the Drupal community, Todd regularly speaks at Drupal events and participates in code sprints all over the world. (In the last four years, he has spoken at 25 conferences and attended five code sprints in seven countries.) Todd is a member of the Drupal Documentation Team and has chaired tracks for DrupalCon Copenhagen 2010, DrupalCon Chicago 2011, DrupalCon Denver 2012, and DrupalCon Munich 2012. He is currently serving as the DrupalCon global chair for all design, user experience, mobile, and theming tracks. As a member of the Drupal.org Redesign Team, Todd helped spearhead the effort to redesign Drupal.org and communicate a fresher, more effective Drupal brand. Todd is also a Certified Scrum Product Owner.

Aaron Stanush

Partner, Co-Founder, Creative Director
Aaron Stanush is a graphic artist and web designer who harbors a special love for logos, branding, and typography. Building large-scale websites occupies most of his time, with most of his efforts guiding sites' aesthetics and user experience. As a member of the Drupal.org Redesign Team, Aaron has participated in several Drupal.org redesign sprints held around the world. Aaron is also a Certified Scrum Product Owner.

Mike Minecki

Director of Technology, Technical Strategist
Mike Minecki has been building websites since 1999. Lately he's focused on building scalable and usable web applications. Before moving to Austin to join the Web Chefs, Mike worked for some of Los Angeles' top interactive agencies on such projects as the1secondfilm.com and sdge.com. When he's not hacking in PHP or JavaScript, he spends his time tinkering on various interactive art projects, snowboarding, scuba diving, and spoiling his dog. Mike has a BFA in Printmaking from California State University Long Beach.

Leah Mason

Director of Client Relations, Marketing Strategist
Leah Mason has a background in marketing, advertising, and project management. Leah has developed campaign strategy for brands like Don't Mess with Texas, Logitech, and Dell. She came to Four Kitchens inspired to collaborate daily with folks who love to learn and is driven by a desire to foster meaningful relationships. The things that make her happy include running the Lady Bird Lake hike-and-bike trail, playing drums loudly, singing loudly, drinking espresso, tap dancing, baking celebratory cakes, and enjoying live music. Leah is a Certified Scrum Master.

Cecy Correa

Director of Marketing and Community Outreach, Social Media and Marketing Strategist
Cecy is a marketer who specializes in social media strategy and engagement, as well as community marketing and outreach. With a background in traditional media, Cecy has worked with for KLRU- Austin, the Discovery Channel, The Austin Film Festival, and The Weinstein Company. She soon moved into interactive marketing at Electronic Arts, where she created and managed online content creation and localization efforts for games like The Sims and Littlest Pet Shop. Cecy has also worked for Dell as an Online Campaign Manager and Social Media Lead for the Channel Marketing group. At Dell, Cecy was part of a training ambassador program, which allowed her to teach social media policy, governance and best practices to professional marketers within the company on a monthly basis. Before joining Four Kitchens, Cecy served as an Account Executive for interactive advertising agency Springbox, where she handled clients ranging from MSN Advertising, the Michael J. Fox Foundation, Dell, and New Era Portfolio. In her spare time, Cecy is part of a core group of organizers for Refresh Austin, one of the largest tech meet-ups in the city.

Suzy Bates

Director of Projects, Project Manager, Scrum Master
Suzy has been managing projects on the web since the words "client/server" described the technology. Since then, she has worked in large organizations and small, as a consultant and inside IT helping people to understand the steps to success often require planning and following the plan, even when things are difficult. A certified Scrum Master, Suzy has experience with many manifestations of "Agile in name only" teams and was relieved to find that Four Kitchens has collaborative, communicative, functional Scrum practices. While holding a Masters of Science in Mechanical Engineering, it has never once gotten her out of an engineering jam. In fact, it has only complicated her abilities to help her sons, ages 8 and 10, in completing their Science Fair projects, as each year she is told that "it's not an Engineering Fair."
